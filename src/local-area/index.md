## About Calpe

Calpe (Calp) is a relaxed seaside town on Spain's Costa Blanca, in the province of Alicante. It's best known for the **Peñón de Ifach**, a dramatic limestone rock rising straight out of the Mediterranean, and for its long sandy beaches, working fishing port, and pretty old town. It's lively in summer and pleasantly quiet out of season, with a great mix of beaches, walking, and food.

## Getting here

- **By air:** The two closest airports are **Alicante–Elche (ALC)**, about a 1-hour drive south, and **Valencia (VLC)**, roughly 1 hour 30 minutes north. Both have car hire and good motorway links.
- **By car:** Calpe sits just off the **AP-7 / A-7 motorway**. There's private parking at the apartment (see *The Apartment*).
- **By train/tram:** The **TRAM Alicante (Line 1)** light rail connects Calpe with Benidorm, Altea and Alicante along the coast.

## The beaches

- **Playa de la Fossa (Levante):** The main beach — wide, sandy, with plenty of restaurants and a promenade.
- **Playa del Arenal-Bol:** A sandy beach right by the old town, with shallow water that's good for families.
- **Cala del Penyal / Cala Calalga:** Smaller coves for a quieter swim and snorkelling.

## Climate

Calpe enjoys a mild Mediterranean climate with over 300 days of sunshine a year. Summers (June–September) are hot and dry — ideal for the beach. Spring and autumn are warm and comfortable for walking and sightseeing, and even winters are gentle.

## Getting around

The town centre, marina and beaches are easily walkable. For trips further afield, car hire gives the most flexibility, but the coastal TRAM and local buses are handy for Benidorm, Altea and Alicante.
